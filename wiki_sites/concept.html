Cloning Protein detection

Proof of Concept

The goal of our project is to establish the modular cloning system (MoClo) in Leishmania tarentolae and to create a genetic part library to facilitate the production of various proteins that were previously difficult to synthesize due to the lack of a suitable organism. L. tarentolae offers the advantage of modifying proteins similar to human cells. This allows the production of human-like proteins, especially for therapeutic purposes. Prioritized was the expression of SARS-CoV-2 receptor binding domain spike protein (RBD).

Cloning

The Golden Gate assembly method based on type II restriction enzymes and T4 ligase was used.

Structure of a level 0 or level 1 destination vector. The L0 vector carries the spectinomycin resistance gene and L1 ampicillin resistance gene.

Following backbones were used to create L0 parts:

  • B2 – pAGM 1276
  • B3 – pICH41258
  • B4 – pAGM 1299
  • B3-B4 – pAGM1287
  • B5 – pAGM 1301

pLEXSY_IE-blecherry3 with LacZ insertion markers was used as a backbone for level 1. The endogenous BsaI interfaces were removed using point mutations. LacZ was used as an indicator of induction for new constructs, allowing blue-white selection in the presence of IPTG and X-Gal. To create matching constructs, BbsI was used at level 0 and BsaI at level 1.

Scheme of the compilation of level 1 from the L0 constructs.

In total. nine constructs were successfully cloned into E. coli and transfected into L. tarentolae.

Scheme of created constructs. 3xHA, TEV-GST, HA-His, Strep8His tags enable the purification of the protein.

Protein detection

Affinity chromatography was used to isolate the target protein from the lyzed L. tarentolae cells.

SARS-CoV-2 RBD isolated from L. tarentolae cultures was detected using CoronaAssay 293T ACE2-TMPRSS2 overexpressing cell line.

Expression of mCherry was detected by fluorescence in the range of 550-650 nm.

Fluorescent L. tarentolae colonies with inserted HA-RBD-Srep8His construct.
